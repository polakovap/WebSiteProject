Taste The Czech Republic!

Let’s take a look at all the delicious traditional foods and drinks you definitely should not missed out on!

Traditional Czech Dishes

Roasted Duck with Cabbage ("Pečená kachna se zelím")

Slow-roasted duck with a golden crispy skin tastes best in wine with braised red cabbage, flavoured with a little currant jam and prunes and mashed potatoes as a side dish.

Sirloin in Cream ("Svíčková na smetaně")

It is a typical Czech dish consisting of boiled beef served with a sauce of root vegetables and yeasted bread dumplings, which are one of the most common side dishes. A slice of lemon, cranberry jam and a dollop of whipped cream on top are not to be missed.

Pork with dumplings and cabbage ("Knedlo vepřo zelo")

For many Czechs, this dish is the most popular. Juicy roasted pork, sweet and sour cabbage and bread or potato dumplings. It's virtually impossible to find a restaurant with Czech cuisine that doesn't offer this classic. Of course, it tastes best with a well-chilled beer.

Goulash ("Guláš")

Although goulash originated in Hungary, it is also considered a typical dish in the Czech Republic. It is served with dumplings or bread. It should be sprinkled with raw onions and, unlike Hungarian beef goulash, Czech beef goulash is thicker and richer. Goulash soup is also very popular.

Fruit Dumplings ("Ovocné knedlíky>")

This dish is sweet and is especially liked by children. Plum dumplings are the most typical, but strawberries and blueberries are also often used and are served mainly in mountainous areas. Fruit dumplings are balls of dough filled with sweet fruit, topped with melted butter and sprinkled with icing sugar together with grated cottage cheese.

Potato & Garlic Soups ("Bramboračka" & "Česnečka")

Soups are indispensable in Czech cuisine. Very popular is potato soup, a thick soup to which potatoes, root vegetables and mushrooms are added. Another traditional recipe is garlic soup. An ideal cure for morning hangovers, it contains garlic, cheese, marjoram, bread croutons and often smoked meat and an egg.

The Tartare ("Tatarák")

A delicacy of raw ground beef with garlic toast, usually served with beer. The tartare is either prepared in the kitchen or served with all the ingredients separately and can be mixed to your own taste. The flavouring is mainly egg yolk, oil, onion, garlic, mustard, ketchup, worcestershire sauce, ground paprika, salt and pepper.

Czech Beer ("Pivo")

Czech beer and brewing has a long tradition and is one of the most famous beers in the world. The oldest documented Czech tradition of brewing beer is the Břevnov Monastery, which was founded in 993. Pilsner Urquell was the first Pilsner-type beer in the world. It is a sin to visit the Czech Republic without tasting the local beer!
